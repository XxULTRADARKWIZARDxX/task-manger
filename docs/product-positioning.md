# Habit Grid Positioning

## Competitor Pattern

Habit trackers are crowded, but most are competing on the same pillars:

- Streaks: Apple-first, streak-focused, colorful, widget-heavy, with Health integration, timed tasks, notes, rich notifications, and many customization options.
  Source: https://apps.apple.com/us/app/streaks/id963034692
- Habitify: cross-platform habit system with reminders, areas/folders, analytics, sync, Health/Fit integrations, challenges, and AI/productivity integrations.
  Source: https://apps.apple.com/us/app/habitify-habit-tracker/id1111447047
- Loop Habit Tracker: Android-only, open source, simple, with reminders, widgets, graphs/statistics, and local-first appeal.
  Source: https://github.com/iSoron/uhabits
- Habitica: gamified task/habit system with avatars, rewards, punishments, quests, and social motivation.
  Source: https://habitica.com/
- Everyday: visual chain board, minimalist habit streaks, skip days, reminders, and browser extension visibility.
  Source: https://everyday.app/features

## Open Space

The clearest gap is not "more reminders" or "more streaks." It is a focused monthly habit ledger:

- Spreadsheet-style rows and columns.
- Black-and-white, print-friendly interface.
- All habits visible in one monthly grid.
- No streak anxiety, avatars, social feed, or decorative productivity theater.

## Positioning Statement

Habit Grid is the paper habit tracker for your phone: a private, black-and-white monthly ledger where you can see every habit and every day at once.

## MVP Differentiators To Keep

- 30-day row grid as the main product, not a secondary analytics screen.
- Grayscale light/dark modes instead of colorful gamification.
- Local-first state and simple daily reminder.
- Simple freemium model: free for 3 rows, $4.99 lifetime unlock for unlimited rows.

## What Not To Build First

- Social competitions.
- RPG rewards.
- AI habit coaching.
- Deep dashboards.
- Subscription-gated core tracking.

Those are already crowded. The launch wedge should be calm, fast, paper-like, and exportable.
